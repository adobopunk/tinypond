The team at Deco Deco approached Tiny Pond with a full service design proposal: for the grand opening of their new LA-based decoden space, they would need a full branding package, and a website that would both share key information with customers and capture their playful pastel aesthetic.

We embraced the challenge and brought their vision to life, handling both branding and web design concurrently, and collaborating closely with the team to tailor every detail to their needs.

  • Brand Identity
  • Web Design

Context

Deco Deco is a charming LA-based decoden studio giding customers through the process of designing and attaching decorative charms to a wide range of products. Decoden is a Japanese art form all about customizing accessories with unique, personalized aesthetics.

Challenge

Build a website and comprehensive design package that includes a logo, color scheme, and overall branding. Deco Deco sought a website that would house all their products and business info while capturing a specific pastel-inspired, whimsical style.

Strategy

By researching direct competitors alongside a general dive into decoden aesthetic, we would aim to capture the kawaii roots of the Japanese art with an LA twist. We would then translate our designs to a user-friendly website experience with products, information, and interactive reservations.

Branding Process

We crafted a playful, cutesy aesthetic that captures Deco Deco's welcoming and happy spirit.

Our branding process began with defining Deco Decoâ€™s playful identity. Our script-based logo, soft color palette, and retro patterns evoke a retro bakery aesthetic with a modern, grounded twist. Every design decision was aimed at building a brand that felt both approachable and magical.

Website Design

By building their web presence, we ensured Deco Deco could communicate key information with customers while demonstrating the fun, pastel aesthetic that defines their business's cheery commitment to a fun customer experience.

With a need to show the store's product offerings, as well as allow the team to keep track of inventory, Shopify was our platform of choice. We heavily customized the platform's "Broadcast" theme to reflect Deco Deco's brand identity.

We introduced visitors immediately to the Decoden concept with a simple three step graphic as the first stop on the home page, followed by a list of available accessories. We build an about page that integrated a Google Maps store locator, a reservations page that used Acuity to allow customers to reserve a time, and designed product pages that allowed for viewing color variations, pricing, and available inventory.
