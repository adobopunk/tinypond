Context

Hot Burger was a live event hosted by Qtcinderella in Los Angeles, inspired by the chaotic fun of the Overcooked video game. Fans placed orders as if they were in the game, while Qtcinderella and her friends cooked and fulfilled them in real time.

Tiny Pond built an event identity that captured the playful, fast-paced energy of Overcooked while delivering a stylized, branded experience for both in-person attendees and online viewers.

What We Did

Taking inspiration from Overcooked, we provided creative direction from start to finish, developing a full suite of design assets to make Hot Burger a success.

A bold, playful logo inspired by Overcooked, with tilted lettering, vibrant colors, and whimsical kitchen theme.

Custom broadcast scenes including multi-scene split boxes, break screens, and lower thirds to keep the live stream fun and engaging.

Eye-catching event signage, live order packaging, and more branded set dressing to enhance the on-site experience.

Design Process

We focused on capturing the chaotic yet fun spirit of Overcooked. We used a wood texture as a unifying theme to create a cozy, kitchen-like atmosphere.

The branding was designed to adapt seamlessly across all event facets—broadcast graphics, signage, merchandise, and more—ensuring a consistent and immersive experience, delighting both in-person attendees and online viewers.

Brand Extensions

To amplify the Hot Burger experience, we extended the brand into a range of merchandise and packaging that attendees could take home:

Playful, collectible stickers featuring the Hot Burger logo, perfect for fans to personalize their belongings and share their love for the event.

Custom-designed aprons for Qtcinderella and her friends not only served a practical purpose but also became a memorable part of the event’s visual identity.

Branded packaging for food orders and merchandise added a cohesive, professional touch to the event’s presentation.
